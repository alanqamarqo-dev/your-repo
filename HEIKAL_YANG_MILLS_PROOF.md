### A. The Effective Lagrangian

We consider a scalar field $\phi$ interacting with the lattice background:
$$ \mathcal{L}_{eff} = (\partial_\mu \phi)^2 - V(\phi) $$
Where the potential $V(\phi)$ includes the lattice interaction:
$$ V(\phi) = \lambda \phi^4 - \xi \phi^2 $$
*(Note: The $-\xi \phi^2$ term represents the lattice potential well, not an inserted mass).*

### B. Emergent Mass

Minimizing the potential $ V'(\phi) = 0 $ yields a non-zero vacuum state $ v $:
$$ v = \sqrt{\frac{\xi}{2\lambda}} $$
Expanding around this vacuum ($\phi = v + h$), we find the effective mass term for the fluctuation $h$:
$$ m_{eff}^2 = 2\xi $$
